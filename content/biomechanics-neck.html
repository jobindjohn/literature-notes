Neck Biomechanics

Neck

  • McElhaney et al. (1988): Constant velocity tests (also upto failure). C1-T1. Two boundary conditions for constant velocity - pinned-pinned and fixed-pinned. Compared with Hybrid III. Moment-rotation curves (also upto failure)
  • Myers et al. (1989): Cervical spine torsion. 6 full cervical spine and 6 lower spine tests.
  • McGill et al. (1994): 40 males, 19 female volunteers (university student population). Flexion, extension, lateral bending.
  • Nightingale et al. (1996): whole unembalmed heads and cervical spines drop at 3.2 ms−1

Cervical Spine Segments

  • Nightingale et al. (2007): 41 male spinal segments (O–C2, C4–C5, C6–C7) in a pure-moment
  • Muth-seng et al. (2019): 9 cadaveric cervical FSUs loaded under 10 Nm flexion moment, relative vertebral and facet rotations and displacements assessed using radio-opaque markers implanted in each vertebra. Cf. Wang et al. (2020)

Invivo Motion studies

  • Yu et al. (2019): 10 asymptomatic subjects imaged using MRI and dual fluoroscopic imaging techniques during extension-flexion and axial left-right rotation. Intervertebral segment CORs and ROMs measured from C34 to C67.
  • Wang et al. (2020): Intervertebral discs and facet joint kinematics. Cf. Muth-seng et al. (2019)
  • Guo et al. (2021): 8 female asymptomatic subjects, Kinematics of the entire head-neck complex (C0-T1) during head axial rotation using a dual fluoroscopic imaging
  • Zhang et al. (2024): 20 asymptomatic subjects, center of rotation (COR) for C2-C7 segments measured using cone beam computed tomography (CBCT)
  • Zhou et al. (2020): C0-T1 kinematics
  • Zhou, Li, et al. (2021): Invivo cervical spine kinematics during walking
  • Schuermans et al. (2024): “aging is associated with loss of the consistent motion pattern that was observed in young asymptomatic individuals”
  • Qu et al. (2024): “Changes in cervical sagittal alignment alter both the quality and quantity of the individual joint motions” Cf. Schuermans et al. (2024)

. Lindenmann et al. (2022): [Review] Kinematics in Healthy and Degenerative Conditions

Ligaments

Upper Cervical Spine

  • Iwanaga et al. (2017): Tensile strength of alar ligaments. Sex Differences
  • Ishak et al. (2021): Alar Ligament, mean failure force 394N +- 52 N
  • Zhou, Guo, et al. (2021): UCS Ligament deformation during axial rotation (biplane fluroscope)
  • Lorente et al. (2022): Kinematics before and after alar ligament transection

Intervertebral Discs

  • Wilson et al. (2021): spatial patterns of deformation (displacements, strain) in adjacent IVDs using dualMRI (displacements under applied loading by MRI) during flexion–extension
